Social Media

Today, Internet users from all over the world utilize and access varieties of social media and Social Network Services (SNS) as a fundamental of their personal networking, relationship collaboration, transferring and sharing of knowledge within communities.

With such a large expansion of communication comes consequences, in which taking the next steps to a safer online environment have become drastically more important.

INSTAGRAM

Cyberbullying in Instagram can happen in different ways, including posting a humiliating image of someone else by perhaps editing the image, posting mean or hateful comments, aggressive captions or hashtags, or creating fake profiles pretending to be someone else-24% of users have reporting being harassed on Instagram.

Instagram CEO Kevin Systrom recently announced as of this past summer, company is "taking the next step," to protect its users from online abuse using a "key moderation tool." This allows users to filter out comments that contain words or phrases they find offensive.

SNAPCHAT

One of the newer platforms to hit mainstream social media, Snapchat, has managed to grow a healthy 150 million daily users. A study done by Ditch the Label, The Wireless Report, states that 7 out of 10 respondents of ages 13-25 who have received online abuse do not report it as they do not think the issue is serious enoough. However, the issues include hacking, death and physical assault threats, and exposure of information.

FACEBOOK

70% of social media users claim that they use Facebook daily. Of these daily Facebook users, 80% of them have said to have experienced online harassment on Facebook. 69% of them knew their harassers in real life, 36% knew them only knew them online, while 25% did not know their harassers at all.

In February of 2016, Facebook responded to these statistics in which it launched a Suicide Prevention tool. The tool works by asking the user to flag and report posts from friends that have caused concern.These posts will then be reviewed by a special team at Facebook, with help options sent to those the reviewers deem to be struggling.
